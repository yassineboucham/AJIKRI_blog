Inspiration Behind Ajikri

Ajikri was born out of the idea to connect people looking to rent a variety of items â€“ from vehicles to real estate and even clothing. The current rental market is often scattered, and Ajikri aims to bring all those elements into a unified platform where users can easily find and list rental ads.

Technology and Architecture

The platform is built using the Laravel framework for backend and Bootstrap for the frontend, ensuring a responsive and scalable user experience. We utilized MySQL for database management and integrated Google Maps for location-based listings.

Core Algorithms

The core of the platform relies on efficient search and filtering algorithms, allowing users to quickly find ads based on category, location, and availability. We optimized the database queries and caching mechanisms to ensure smooth performance even as the user base grows.

Collaboration and Timeline

Our team consisted of developers, data collectors, and marketers. Using Agile methodologies, we broke down the project into manageable sprints, regularly testing and adjusting features to align with user feedback. Tools like Trello and GitHub were essential in tracking our progress.

Challenges and Learnings

Building Ajikri came with its fair share of challenges, particularly in integrating Google Maps and ensuring optimal performance with dynamic data loading. Through these obstacles, we learned a great deal about web performance optimization and the importance of user-centered design.
